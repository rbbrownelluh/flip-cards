Secondary Source Flip Cards

Secondary Sources For Getting Started

Encyclopedias

  • Broad overview of all legal topics arranged in alphabetical order
  • May be general or jurisdictionally-specific
  • Provide citations to primary law
  • Regularly updated

Dictionaries

  • Find non-binding legal definitions of terms & phrases
  • May include references to primary law
  • May point researchers to related terminology

Treatises

  • In-depth, scholarly treatment of a single broad area of law (ex. torts)
  • Provide citations to primary law
  • May be general or jurisdictionally-specific
  • May or may not be updated

Specialized Secondary Sources

Law Reviews

  • Articles on a narrow topic written by scholars & practitioners
  • Often discuss novel ideas & current developments in the law
  • Provide citations to primary & other secondary law
  • Never updated, so can become outdated

American Law Reports

  • In-depth annotations on a specific, narrow topic
  • Include comprehensive list of citations across jurisdictions
  • Do not cover every topic, so not available for every issue
  • Regularly updated

Restatements

  • Written by group of experts to indicate what law “should” be
  • Include comments & illustrations of how rules should be applied
  • Not indicative of the law in any particular jurisdiction
  • Jurisdictions can adopt their language by statute or common law

Form Books

  • Include sample documents or templates
  • Used to help guide drafting of legal documents
  • May or may not be updated

Practice Guides

  • Created for practitioners
  • Focus on practical tips & process
  • Often provide citations to primary law
